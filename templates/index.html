    Data Analysis?

    Welcome to our page! Scroll down to learn more about data job listings.

  • What's the difference?

    • Data Analyst

      They typically help people from across the company understand specific quieries with charts.

    • Data Engineer

      They are versatile generalists who use computer science to help process large datasets. They typically focus on coding, cleaning up data sets, and implementing requests that come from data scientists.

    • Data Scientist

      A data scientist will be able to take data science projects from end to end. They can help store large amounts of data, create predictive modelling processes and present the findings.

  • Salary Analysis by Job Type

    We have found that the majority of the job listings offer at least a $100K a year. A significant majority of jobs that offer more the $100K a year are Data Science jobs whereas the majority of jobs that offer less than $100k are Data analysts jobs.

  • What Industries are Looking to Hire You?

    Consulting and business service companies are the top landing spot for most folks in the Data Jobs Market. With Banking and Financial Services and Internet and Software companies falling in line in second, and third place respectively, it's not surprising that these Data Jobs pay so well.

  • What Skills Do You Need?

    As the preeminent data management language, SQL is the most important coding language for employment candidates seeking positions as Data Engineers and Data Analysts, although it is only a distant fourth for those seeking employment as Data Scientists. For these folks, Python is the most important language due to its wide variety of statistical functions and deep selection of coding libraries.
